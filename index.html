Abstract

Large Language Models (LLMs) show great promise in automated survey generation, yet several bottlenecks remain: (1) static retrieval fails to mimic human scholars; (2) context overflow; and (3) training difficulties in multi-agent systems.

We introduce Agent Survey, a multi-agent framework that emulates human workflows. To optimize this without human-annotated data, we introduce Pipeline RL, which establishes a hierarchical information flow across agents. Furthermore, we propose Vectorized Reward Policy Optimization (VRPO) to stabilize dual-objective learning, balancing semantic depth and syntactic (LaTeX) quality. Extensive evaluations show Agent Survey outperforms existing methods and closely aligns with human standards.

Core Innovations

VRPO Algorithm: Replaces scalar rewards with vectorized advantage aggregation to balance "Content Depth" vs "LaTeX Integrity".

Pipeline RL: Resolves the lack of intermediate data by using upstream agent outputs as dynamic RL states.

User Preference: Agent Survey ranked 1st or 2nd by 60% of human experts, significantly higher than other AI baselines.
